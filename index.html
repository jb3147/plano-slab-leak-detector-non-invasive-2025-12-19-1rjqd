Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our non-invasive slab leak detection service in Plano, from how we work to what to expect on the day of your appointment.

We use a combination of specialized tools to “listen” and “see” through the slab rather than cutting into it. Acoustic listening devices amplify the sound of pressurized water escaping from a pipe, tracer gas testing can reveal leaks in complex or concealed lines, and thermal imaging highlights heat patterns created by hot water leaks. By cross-referencing these readings, we can narrow the leak to a small area under the slab with a high degree of confidence—without removing flooring or concrete during the detection process.
Most Plano slab leak detections take between 45 and 90 minutes, depending on the size of the property, the complexity of the plumbing layout, and how obvious the symptoms are. We start with a brief walkthrough to understand what you’re noticing—warm spots, sounds, high bills, visible moisture—then perform targeted testing on the suspected lines. Before we leave, we review our findings with you, show you any relevant readings or images, and mark the suspected leak location for your plumber or repair contractor.
We specialize exclusively in detection, not repair. This focus allows us to remain objective and concentrate on providing the most accurate, non-invasive diagnostics possible. After we locate the suspected leak, we clearly mark the area and can provide a written summary of our findings. You may then share that information with your preferred plumber, foundation specialist, or restoration company. Many local contractors appreciate receiving our reports because it shortens their troubleshooting time and reduces the amount of concrete or flooring they need to disturb.
Coverage varies by policy, but many homeowners insurance plans will consider slab leak detection and access costs when there is active damage to the structure. We are not an insurance company and cannot guarantee coverage; however, we can provide detailed documentation of our findings—including suspected leak location, evidence, and photos—that you may submit to your adjuster. In Plano, we regularly work with homeowners who are in the middle of an insurance claim and need professional confirmation of a suspected slab leak.
Before we arrive, it helps to clear any furniture, rugs, or storage items away from areas where you’ve noticed symptoms—such as warm spots, dampness, or cracking. If you have access to your water shutoff, meter, or previous plumbing diagrams, have those handy as well. Please avoid running large amounts of water right before your appointment, as we may need to perform specific pressure or meter tests. When we arrive, we’ll walk through your observations together and outline exactly what we’ll be doing so there are no surprises.
